    Research Papers

    Below are some of the research papers I've worked on in the fields of Data Science, Machine Learning, and Big Data Analytics.

    Leveraging Big Data for Disease Surveillance and Public Health Interventions (IJGIS July 2024) (Published)

    Big data analytics enhances disease surveillance and public health by enabling timely outbreak detection, predictive modeling, and targeted interventions, though challenges like data quality and privacy must be addressed. Strengthening data integration, protection, and interdisciplinary collaboration is essential for maximizing its impact.

    AI-Enhanced U-Net for Accurate Low-Grade Glioma Segmentation in Brain MRI:Transforming Healthcare Imaging(ICEEICT 2024)

    This paper presents an end-to-end U-Net deep neural network for automatically segmenting low-grade glioma (LGG) regions in MR images, demonstrating high accuracy and performance metrics like the dice coefficient and intersection over union (IoU). The results suggest that Deep Learning-based tools like U-Net can significantly assist radiologists in brain tumor segmentation.

    Beyond Words: Exploring Emotion Detection in Speech Using Sound(INCET 2024)

    This paper explores the application of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for Speech Emotion Recognition (SER) using the TESS dataset, achieving optimized performance through rigorous preprocessing and hyperparameter tuning. The findings underscore LSTM's effectiveness in capturing audio data dependencies and its potential for advancing SER applications.

    A Study on Integrating Machine Learning Techniques for Waste Management(ICCPCT 2024)

    This study examines the use of various machine learning algorithms in waste management, with the Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) classifier achieving the highest accuracy of 91.81%. The findings highlight the effectiveness of machine learning approaches in improving waste classification tasks.

    Unlocking Healthcare Fraud Detection Using Innovations of Machine Learning Strategies(IDBA 2024)

    This paper evaluates the effectiveness of machine learning classifiers, including Random Forest, SVM, and KNN, for detecting healthcare insurance fraud. Random Forest outperforms SVM and KNN with accuracies of 82% and 80%, respectively, highlighting its suitability for enhancing fraud detection and cost efficiency in healthcare services.
